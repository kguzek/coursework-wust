### New vocabulary – examples

| Japanese                            | English                         |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Watashi wa mise ni ikimas           | I am going to the shop          |
| Anata wa mise ni ikimas ka?         | Are you going to the shop?      |
| Iie, watashi wa mise niwa ikimasen. | No, I am not going to the shop. |
| Anata wa doko ni ikimas ka?         | Where are you going?            |
| Anata wa doko ni ima ikimas ka?     | Where are you going tomorrow?   |

## New grammar

1. The negation of `x wa y ni imas` is `x wa y niwa imasen`. It can only be used in true negation sentences, such as for expressing guilt or incorrectness.
2. The infinitive form of verbs ends with `u`.

### New grammar – examples

| Japanese                         | English                    |
| -------------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Watashi wa ie niwa imasen.       | I am not home.             |
| Sakana wa doubutsuen ni imas ka? | Are there fish in the zoo? |
