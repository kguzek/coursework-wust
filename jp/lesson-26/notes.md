## New grammar

1. `Sake` can be used to refer to alcohol in general, not just Japanese rice wine.
2. Transformation rules: if a verb's infinitive ends with `mu`, the present tense form ends with `mimas` instead. There are three verb groups:
   1. (Potentially) irregulars: iru -> imas, aru -> arimas, iku -> ikimas, kuru -> kimas, suru -> shimas
   2. Weak `-ru` ending (replaced with `-mas`): taberu -> tabemas
   3. General rule (`-u` ending changes to `-imas`): kaeru -> kaerimas, kaku -> kakimas, tatsu -> tachimas

### New grammar – examples

| Japanese                   | English                                   |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Watashi wa sake o nomimas. | I drank sake (or any alcoholic beverage). |
